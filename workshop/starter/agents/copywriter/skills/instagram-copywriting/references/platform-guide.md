# Instagram Platform Guide

## Character limits

| Element | Limit | Recommended |
|---------|-------|-------------|
| Caption | 2,200 chars | 138-150 chars for feed visibility before fold |
| Hashtags | 30 max | 5-10 (optimal engagement) |
| Username mentions | 20 max | Use sparingly |

## The fold

On mobile, captions truncate after ~125 characters and a "...more" link appears. The **hook must land before this line**. Everything after the fold is for engaged readers only.

## Hashtag strategy

Mix three tiers for maximum reach without looking spammy:

- **Broad (1-3 tags):** 1M+ posts - `#wellness`, `#fitness`, `#skincare`
- **Mid-tier (2-4 tags):** 100K-1M posts - `#sustainableliving`, `#cleaneating`
- **Niche (2-4 tags):** under 100K posts - most likely to surface in "following" hashtag feeds

Place hashtags at the end of the caption after a line break or period. Do not weave them into the sentence mid-copy.

## Algorithm signals (as of 2024-2025)

- **Save rate** is the strongest engagement signal - write copy that makes people want to save for later ("Save this before your next gym session")
- **Comment depth** ranks over comment count - open-ended questions outperform yes/no prompts
- **Shares to Stories** are weighted heavily - relatable or surprising content drives this
- **Watch time on Reels** matters more than likes - for video captions, front-load the hook
- Posting velocity: 4-7 feed posts per week is the sweet spot for most brand accounts

## Tone calibration for Instagram

Instagram skews aspirational, not transactional. Even urgency-tone captions should feel like a tip from a friend, not a sales pitch. Avoid:
- Exclamation mark overuse (max 1-2 per caption)
- All-caps (except one deliberate word for emphasis)
- Emoji spam (0-3 is professional; >5 reads as low-effort)
